Training transcript tips

Listen to the audio and fix the text to match what was actually said. ALL CAPS is fine — the transcript collector converts everything to lowercase for training.

  • Numbers as digits — addresses, units, plates, times, codes. forty two main street → 42 main st
  • Streets & places — check spelling of roads, cities, landmarks, highways. brodway & magnolia av → broadway & magnolia ave
  • Highways & routes — use digits for route numbers. interstate seventy one north → i-71 north state route three fifteen → sr 315
  • Sound-alike words — pick the word that fits dispatch context. white mail suspect → white male suspect two units two scene → two units to scene en route two the seen → en route to the scene red break lights → red brake lights
  • Unit & radio IDs — use digits when spoken as numbers. engine twelve → engine 12
  • License plates — letters and digits exactly as read on the air. plate oh four seven kay en → plate oh 047 kn
  • 10-codes & signals — match how they're spoken on your system. ten fifty → 10-50
  • Medical & fire terms — spell out what was said; don't abbreviate unless they did. gsw to the leg → gunshot wound to the leg
  • Only what's spoken — remove tones, beeps, or hallucinated filler; don't add words that aren't in the audio.
